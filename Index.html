History of Dharmshilla Narayana Superspeciality Hospital

Dharmshilla Narayana Superspeciality Hospital, located in Delhi, India, has been a beacon of hope for patients since its inception. The hospital was originally established as Dharmshilla Hospital and Research Centre in 1994. Over the years, it has grown to become one of the leading healthcare providers in the region, offering state-of-the-art medical care across various specialties.

The hospital was founded with the vision of providing high-quality healthcare services at an affordable cost. It has always been at the forefront of medical innovation, equipped with advanced technology and staffed by experienced medical professionals dedicated to patient care.

Complex Cases Handled by the Hospital

Dharmshilla Narayana Superspeciality Hospital has successfully managed numerous complex medical cases, earning a reputation for excellence in patient care. Some of the notable cases include:

  • Case 1:Advanced Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer with Metasta

    • Patient Background:

    A 58-year-old male presented with persistent cough, shortness of breath, and unexplained weight loss. He was a long-term smoker with a history of chronic bronchitis.

    Diagnosis:

    Imaging studies, including a CT scan and PET scan, revealed a large mass in the right lung with metastases to the lymph nodes and liver. A biopsy confirmed the diagnosis of Stage IV Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC), specifically adenocarcinoma.

    Treatment:

    The oncology team at Dharmshilla Narayana Superspeciality Hospital devised a personalized treatment plan involving a combination of targeted therapy and immunotherapy. He was started on Erlotinib, a tyrosine kinase inhibitor targeting the EGFR mutation identified in his tumor, along with Nivolumab, an immune checkpoint inhibitor.

    Outcome:

    After six months of treatment, follow-up imaging showed a significant reduction in the size of the primary tumor and metastatic lesions. His symptoms improved markedly, and he experienced fewer side effects compared to traditional chemotherapy. He continues to receive maintenance therapy and regular monitoring.

  • Case 2:Small Cell Lung Cancer with Brain Metastasis

    • Patient Background:

    A 65-year-old female, presented with severe headaches, dizziness, and difficulty in coordination. She had a history of smoking but had quit 20 years prior.

    Diagnosis:

    MRI of the brain showed multiple lesions suggestive of metastases. A subsequent CT scan of the chest revealed a small mass in the left lung. A biopsy confirmed Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC), and the brain lesions were determined to be metastatic.

    Treatment:

    Given the aggressive nature of SCLC, she was started on a regimen of chemotherapy with Cisplatin and Etoposide. Concurrently, she underwent whole-brain radiotherapy to address the brain metastases.

    Outcome:

    After completing the initial rounds of chemotherapy and radiotherapy, follow-up scans indicated a partial response with a reduction in the size of both the primary lung tumor and brain metastases. Her neurological symptoms improved significantly, and she continues to receive maintenance chemotherapy with close neurological monitoring.

  • Case 3: Early-Stage Lung Cancer Treated with Surgery

    • Patient Background:

Acquisition by Narayana Health

In 2017, Dharmshilla Hospital and Research Centre was acquired by Narayana Health, one of India's largest and most respected healthcare providers. The acquisition marked a new chapter in the hospital's history, leading to its rebranding as Dharmshilla Narayana Superspeciality Hospital.

This strategic move has significantly enhanced the hospital's capabilities, allowing it to leverage Narayana Health's extensive network and resources. It has also facilitated the introduction of new specialties and advanced medical treatments, further cementing the hospital's status as a leading healthcare institution.

Under the Narayana Health umbrella, Dharmshilla Narayana Superspeciality Hospital continues to provide exceptional medical care, staying true to its mission of delivering world-class healthcare services to patients.
